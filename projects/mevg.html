MEVG: Multi-Event Video Generation with Text-to-Video Models

Gyeongrok Oh, Jaehwan Jeong, Sieun Kim, Wonmin Byeon, Jinkyu Kim, Sungwoong Kim, Sangpil Kim

European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV), 2024

ECCV Paper arXiv GitHub

Abstract

Text-to-video generation models typically condition on a single text prompt, limiting their ability to synthesize videos that involve multiple distinct events unfolding over time. MEVG addresses this by enabling multi-event video generation: given a sequence of text descriptions, the model produces a temporally coherent video in which each segment faithfully reflects its corresponding event. We propose a framework that coordinates multiple text-conditioned generation stages while maintaining visual continuity across event boundaries, resulting in smooth, semantically rich video narratives.

Key Contributions

  • Multi-event video generation framework conditioned on a sequence of text prompts.
  • Temporal coordination across generation stages to ensure visual continuity between events.
  • Semantically faithful synthesis for each event segment without cross-contamination.
  • Evaluation on multi-event benchmarks showing improved temporal coherence and semantic alignment.
