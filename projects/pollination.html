Vision-Guided Targeted Grasping and Vibration for Robotic Pollination in Controlled Environments

Jaehwan Jeong*, Tuan-Anh Vu*, Radha Lahoti, Jiawen Wang, Vivek Alumootil, Sangpil Kim, M. Khalid Jawed
* Equal contribution

Project period: 2025.08.

arXiv

Abstract

Crop pollination is a critical agricultural task that is increasingly difficult to sustain with declining pollinator populations. We present an end-to-end robotic system for autonomous tomato pollination in controlled greenhouse environments. Our approach combines vision-guided flower detection, targeted grasping with a custom end-effector, and mechanical vibration to simulate natural bee pollination. The system achieves reliable flower identification and manipulation, demonstrating a practical path toward scalable automated crop pollination.

Key Contributions

  • End-to-end robotic pipeline for autonomous tomato pollination in greenhouse settings.
  • Vision-guided flower detection and targeted grasping with a specialized end-effector.
  • Mechanical vibration strategy replicating natural bee buzz pollination.
  • Comprehensive evaluation of pollination success rates and robustness to environmental variation.
