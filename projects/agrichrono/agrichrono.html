AgriChrono: A Multi-modal Dataset Capturing Crop Growth and Lighting Variability with a Field Robot

TL;DR

We propose AgriChrono, a comprehensive framework purpose-built for "in-the-wild" scene-level 3D reconstruction, featuring three core contributions: a custom robotic platform, an 18 TB multi-modal dataset, and a stress-test benchmark. First, we deploy a modular robotic platform equipped with a highly accurate, time-synchronized sensor suite (LiDAR-RGB-Depth-IMU) to continuously log the high-fidelity 6-DoF poses and structural data required for reliable 3D reconstruction. Second, utilizing this platform, we release a massive dataset that rigorously captures continuous morphological crop growth and severe daily illumination shifts over a month. Finally, we establish a 7-scenario 3D reconstruction benchmark to evaluate 16 state-of-the-art NeRF and 3DGS methods. While validating our platform's data-gathering efficacy, this benchmark explicitly exposes critical robustness issues in existing baselines, demonstrating their failure to maintain consistent performance under wind-induced crop motion and lighting variations. Ultimately, AgriChrono provides a crucial stress-test to catalyze the development of robust, next-generation in-the-wild 3D reconstruction models.

Key Contributions

  • Modular Robotic Acquisition Platform — We develop a custom, remote-operable field robot equipped with a precise, time-synchronized multi-modal sensor suite (RGB, Depth, LiDAR, IMU, and Pose). This platform serves as a foundational tool, validating that high-fidelity 3D reconstruction is achievable in challenging, "in-the-wild" agricultural environments.
  • Large-Scale In-the-Wild Dataset — We release the comprehensive 18 TB AgriChrono dataset, uniquely capturing continuous morphological crop growth and systematic daily illumination changes. Collected daily over a one-month period, this dataset documents complex, scene-level structural changes to significantly accelerate real-world precision agriculture research.
  • 3D Reconstruction Stress-Test Benchmark — We introduce a rigorous benchmark featuring 7 distinct scenarios to evaluate 16 state-of-the-art NeRF and 3DGS methods. This benchmark illustrates the significant challenges of non-rigid, dynamic scenes, explicitly exposing the fundamental robustness limitations of existing baselines against wind-induced motion and lighting shifts.

Project Design

1. Field & Remote Operation

The collection site and tele-operation setup enabling ~1,500-mile remote data collection from UCLA to NDSU.

Overview of the data collection environment and remote operation pipeline. The greenhouse facility at NDSU (left) hosts rows of tomato and pepper crops spanning multiple growing stages. A custom WebUI (right) provides real-time camera feeds, robot state visualization, and teleoperation controls, enabling the entire 3-week collection campaign to be conducted remotely from UCLA — approximately 1,500 miles away — without requiring on-site operators.

2. Robot Hardware Design

Sensor suite and hardware configuration of the field robot platform.

Hardware configuration of the field robot. The platform mounts two ZED stereo cameras (forward- and downward-facing) for wide-baseline RGB-D capture, and a Livox LiDAR for high-fidelity point cloud acquisition. An Obsbot PTZ camera provides an independent top-down monitoring view, supporting reliable remote supervision during the tele-operation sessions. Additional sensors include an IMU and wheel encoders for odometry.

Remote tele-operation setup at UCLA. The operator controls the field robot deployed at NDSU's greenhouse facilities approximately 1,500 miles away via a custom-built WebUI, enabling consistent data collection without on-site personnel.

3. Data Collection Protocol

Longitudinal capture schedule: 4 sessions per day over 3 weeks, with multi-sensor time synchronization.

Data collection schedule and modality overview. The robot traversed the greenhouse 4 times per day at fixed intervals (morning, noon, afternoon, evening), capturing the full range of natural lighting variability under greenhouse conditions. Over 3 weeks, this yields a dense temporal record of crop growth dynamics — from seedling to flowering stage. All sensors are hardware time-synchronized to sub-millisecond accuracy, ensuring consistent multi-modal alignment across RGB, depth, and LiDAR streams.

4. 3D Reconstruction Benchmark

AgriChrono's multi-view coverage enables a rigorous benchmark for 3D reconstruction in agricultural scenes.

The synchronized multi-view data from AgriChrono supports a comprehensive 3D reconstruction benchmark. Dense point clouds derived from the Livox LiDAR serve as ground truth geometry, while the ZED stereo pairs provide photometric reference for novel view synthesis evaluation. The dataset captures unique challenges absent in standard benchmarks: fine leaf geometry, specular reflections under greenhouse lighting, and temporal deformation as plants grow across sessions.
