AgriChrono: A Multi-modal Dataset Capturing Crop Growth and Lighting Variability with a Field Robot

Jaehwan Jeong, Tuan-Anh Vu, Mohammad Jony, Shahab Ahmad, Md. Mukhlesur Rahman, Sangpil Kim, M. Khalid Jawed

Under review, 2026

arXiv GitHub Project Page

Abstract

Understanding long-term crop dynamics requires rich, multi-modal temporal data collected in real agricultural environments. AgriChrono is a large-scale dataset captured by an autonomous field robot deployed in greenhouse settings over extended growing seasons. The dataset spans RGB imagery, depth sensing, thermal imaging, and LiDAR point clouds, synchronized with environmental metadata such as lighting conditions, humidity, and temperature. AgriChrono enables research in temporal scene understanding, multi-modal fusion, and agricultural AI for precision farming.

Key Contributions

  • Large-scale multi-modal dataset collected longitudinally with a field robot.
  • Captures crop growth stages, lighting variability, and environmental conditions.
  • Synchronized RGB, depth, thermal, and LiDAR modalities with environmental metadata.
  • Benchmark tasks for temporal crop understanding and multi-modal fusion.
