FaceShield: Defending Facial Image against Deepfake Threats

Jaehwan Jeong, Sumin In, Sieun Kim, Hannie Shin, Jongheon Jeong, Sang Ho Yoon, Jaewook Chung, Sangpil Kim

International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV), 2025

ICCV Paper arXiv GitHub

Abstract

Deepfake technology poses a growing threat to personal identity and digital trust. FaceShield is a proactive defense framework that protects facial images before they are misused. By embedding imperceptible adversarial perturbations into facial images, FaceShield disrupts deepfake synthesis pipelines—including face-swapping and face re-enactment models—without any visible quality degradation to the original image. Our approach generalizes across a wide range of deepfake generators and demonstrates robustness to common image transformations encountered in social media pipelines.

Key Contributions

  • Proactive adversarial perturbation framework for facial image protection against deepfake threats.
  • Imperceptible perturbations that disrupt both face-swapping and re-enactment models.
  • Generalizes to unseen deepfake generators without access to the target model.
  • Robust to JPEG compression, resizing, and other social-media transformations.
